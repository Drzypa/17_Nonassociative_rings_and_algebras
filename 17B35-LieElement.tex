Given a Lie algebra $\mathfrak{g}$ and its univeral enveloping algebra
$\mathfrak{U(g)}$, then an element $x\in \mathfrak{U(g)}$ is called
a \emph{Lie element} if $x$ is in the image of $\mathfrak{g}$ in $\mathfrak{U(g)}$.

This term is most often used for free Lie algebras and specifically in 
the famous Baker-Campbell-Hausdorff formula (theorem).
