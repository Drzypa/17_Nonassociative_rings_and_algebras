Jacobi identity interpretations


The Jacobi identity in a Lie algebra 𝔤 has various interpretations that are more transparent, whence easier to remember, than the usual form

[x,[y,z]]+[y,[z,x]]+[z,[x,y]]=0.

One is the fact that the adjoint representation 11Here, “𝔤⁢𝔩⁢(𝔤)” means the space o endomorphisms of 𝔤, viewed as a vector space, with Lie bracket on 𝔤⁢𝔩⁢(𝔤)being commutator. ad:𝔤→𝔤⁢𝔩⁢(𝔤) really is a representation. Yet another way to formulate the identity is

ad⁡(x)⁢[y,z]=[ad⁡(x)⁢y,z]+[y,ad⁡(x)⁢z],

i.e., ad⁡(x) is a derivation on 𝔤 for all x∈𝔤.
