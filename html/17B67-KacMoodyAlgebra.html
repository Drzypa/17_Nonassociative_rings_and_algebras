Kac-Moody algebra


Let A be an n×n generalized Cartan matrix. If n-r is the rank of A, then let 𝔥 be a n+r dimensional complex vector space. Choose n linearly independent elements α1,…,αn∈𝔥* (called roots), and αˇ1,…,αˇn∈𝔥 (called coroots) such that ⟨αi,αjˇ⟩=ai⁢j, where ⟨⋅,⋅⟩ is the natural pairing of 𝔥* and 𝔥. This choice is unique up to automorphisms of 𝔥.

Then the Kac-Moody algebra associated to 𝔤⁢(A) is the Lie algebra generated by elements X1,…,Xn,Y1,…,Yn and the elements of 𝔥, with the relations

[Xi,Yi] =αiˇ [Xi,Yj] =0
=αi⁢(h)⁢Xi [Yi,h] =-αi⁢(h)⁢Yi
[Xi,[Xi,⋯,[Xi⏟1-ai⁢j⁢ times,Xj]⋯]] =0 [Yi,[Yi,⋯,[Yi⏟1-ai⁢j⁢ times,Yj]⋯]] =0

for any h∈𝔥.

If the matrix A is positive-definite, we obtain a finite dimensional semi-simple Lie algebra, and A is the Cartan matrix associated to a Dynkin diagram. Otherwise, the algebra we obtain is infinite dimensional and has an r-dimensional center.
