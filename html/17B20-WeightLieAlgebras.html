weight (Lie algebras)


Let 𝔥 be an abelian Lie algebra, V a vector space and ρ:𝔥→End⁡V a representation. Then the representation is said to be diagonalisable, if V can be written as a direct sum

V=⊕λ∈𝔥*Vλ

where 𝔥* is the dual space of 𝔥 and

Vλ={v∈V∣ρ⁢(h)⁢v=λ⁢(h)⁢v⁢ for all ⁢h∈𝔥}.

Now let 𝔤 be a semi-simple Lie algebra. Fix a Cartan subalgebra 𝔥, then 𝔥 is abelian. Let ρ:𝔤→End⁡V be a representation whose restriction to 𝔥 is diagonalisable. Then for any λ∈𝔥*, the space Vλ is the weight space of λ with respect to ρ. The multiplicity of λ with respect to ρ is the dimension of Vλ:

multρ⁡(λ):=dim⁡Vλ.

If the multiplicity of λ is greater than zero, then λ is called a weight of the representation ρ.

A representation of a semi-simple Lie algebra is determined by the multiplicities of its weights.
