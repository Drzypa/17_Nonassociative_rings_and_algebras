classification of finite-dimensional representations of semi-simple Lie algebras


If 𝔤 is a semi-simple Lie algebra, then we say that an representation V has highest weight λ, if there is a vector v∈Vλ, the weight space of λ, such that X⁢v=0 for X in any positive root space, and v is called a highest vector, or vector of highest weight.

There is a unique (up to isomorphism) irreducible finite dimensional representation of 𝔤 with highest weight λ for any dominant weight λ∈ΛW, where ΛW is the weight lattice of 𝔤, and every irreducible representation of 𝔤 is of this type.
