flexible algebra


A non-associative algebra A is flexible if [a,b,a]=0 for all a,b∈A, where [,,] is the associator on A. In other words, we have (a⁢b)⁢a=a⁢(b⁢a) for all a,b∈A. Any associative algebra is clearly flexible. Furthermore, any alternative algebra with characteristic ≠2 is flexible.

Given an element a in a flexible algebra A, define the left power of a iteratively as follows:

  1. 1.

    L1⁢(a)=a,

  2. 2.

    Ln⁢(a)=a⋅Ln-1⁢(a).

Similarly, we can define the right power of a as:

  1. 1.

    R1⁢(a)=a,

  2. 2.

    Rn⁢(a)=Rn-1⁢(a)⋅a.

Then, we can show that Ln⁢(a)=Rn⁢(a) for all positive integers n. As a result, in a flexible algebra, one can define the (multiplicative) power of an element a as an unambiguously.
