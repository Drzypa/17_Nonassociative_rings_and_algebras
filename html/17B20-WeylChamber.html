Weyl chamber


Let E be a Euclidean vector space, R⊂E a root system, and R+⊂R a choice of positive roots. We define the positive Weyl chamber (relative to R+) to be the closed set

𝒞={u∈E∣(u,α)≥0⁢ for all ⁢α∈R+}.

A weight which lies inside the positive Weyl chamber is called dominant.

The interior of 𝒞 is a fundamental domain for the action of the Weyl group on E. The image w⁢(𝒞) of 𝒞 under the any element w of the Weyl group is called a Weyl chamber. The Weyl group W acts simply transitively on the set of Weyl chambers.
